Prepositions

In

Time

Use Example
months Paris is wonderful in April.
years I first went to Russia in 2005.
seasons We often go skiing in winter.
parts of the day My train leaves in the afternoon.

Place

Use Example
towns and cities There's a famous castle in Edinburgh.
countries and continents My brother is in Mexico.
areas and regions What's life like in the desert?
inside an object Your passport is in the drawer.
inside a room I've left the tickets in the living room!
inside a building Sharon has been in the travel agent's for an hour!
Helpful hints: We also use in in the following phrases:
  • in a minute/an hour – I'll call you back in a minute.
  • in front of – She was standing in front of the school.
  • in the middle (of) – He was in the middle of a meeting when I called.
  • in the future – I hope to travel more in the future.
Grammar Rule: Use in to indicate a point of time in the future or a position inside a space/area.
  • The show will start in an hour.
  • The cat is in the box.
  • She plans to learn French in the future.
Watch out! With verbs of motion (come, go, move, run, walk, etc.), we usually use to instead of in, on, or at.
Examples:
  • She is going to the park.
  • We moved to London last year.
  • He ran to the shop to buy milk.

On

Time

Use Example
days I got a new car on Saturday.
dates My birthday is on 19th March.

Place

Use Example
islands Last year, we stayed on Mykonos.
pages There are some useful Italian phrases on page 97.
on top of an object Did you put your car keys on the kitchen table?
on a surface There's a timetable on the wall.
Helpful hints: We also use on in the following phrases:
  • on the beach – We had a picnic on the beach.
  • on the left/right – The bookstore is on the left of the cinema.
  • on my birthday – I received a lot of gifts on my birthday.
Grammar Rule: Use on for surfaces, days/dates, and positions relative to something.
  • The keys are on the table.
  • I met her on Monday.
  • The picture is on the wall.
Watch out!
  • We say in the morning/afternoon/evening, but on Monday morning/Wednesday evening.
  • We don't use a preposition with tomorrow, yesterday, etc.
Examples:
  • I will call you on Monday morning.
  • We're flying to Washington tomorrow afternoon.
  • She will visit us on Friday evening.

At

Time

Use Example
clock times There's a bus at ten past three.
holiday periods What are you doing at Christmas?

Place

Use Example
exact places What's it like at the North Pole?
addresses My cousin lives at 132 London Road.
buildings (activities) I think John is at the cinema, watching Titanic.
activities Rania isn't here. She's at a party.
Helpful hints: We also use at in the following phrases:
  • at the moment – I'm busy at the moment.
  • at night – We usually go for a walk at night.
  • at the top/bottom – The elevator stopped at the top floor.
  • at the door/window – She was waiting at the door for me.
Grammar Rule: Use at for small points of time or exact locations.
  • We meet at 6 pm every Friday.
  • He is waiting at the bus stop.
  • The kids are playing at the park entrance.
Watch out! Compare how we use in and at for places.
We use in for larger areas that are all around us, and at for smaller places and points on a journey.
Examples:
  • We're spending our next holiday in the countryside.
  • Let's meet at the train station.
  • He lives in London, but we met at London Bridge.
