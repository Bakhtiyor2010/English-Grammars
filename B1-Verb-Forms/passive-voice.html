Passive Voice

Uses & Examples

When we don't know who does something

  • My bike was stolen last night.
  • The window is broken.
  • The documents have been lost.
  • The road is being repaired.
  • The package will be delivered tomorrow.

When we don't want or don't need to say who does something

  • English is spoken all over the world.
  • The results were announced yesterday.
  • The project has been completed.
  • The house is being painted.
  • The work will be finished soon.

By / With

We use by to emphasise who does something

  • The novel was written by George Orwell.
  • The cake was made by my sister.
  • The song was performed by a famous singer.
  • The project was completed by the students.
  • The decision was made by the manager.

We use with to emphasise what someone uses

  • The door was opened with a key.
  • The picture was painted with a brush.
  • The letter was written with a pen.
  • The window was broken with a stone.
  • The vegetables were cut with a knife.

We don't use by or with when we don't need to say, or don't know, who does something.

  • My phone was stolen.
  • The road is being repaired.
  • The results were announced.
  • The house has been built.
  • The homework will be checked tomorrow.

Modals (might / should / must / can)

Modal Structure
Can (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + can + be + verb 3
Should (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + should + be + verb 3
Must (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + must + be + verb 3
Might (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + might + be + verb 3

Can

  • The problem can be solved easily.
  • The task can be completed today.
  • The door can be opened from inside.
  • The report can be printed now.
  • The mistake can be corrected.

Must

  • The rules must be followed.
  • The door must be locked at night.
  • The form must be signed.
  • The homework must be finished today.
  • The instructions must be read carefully.

Should

  • The homework should be completed on time.
  • The problem should be discussed.
  • The report should be checked again.
  • The room should be cleaned.
  • The decision should be reconsidered.

Might

  • The plan might be changed.
  • The meeting might be postponed.
  • The results might be announced tomorrow.
  • The project might be delayed.
  • The road might be closed.
