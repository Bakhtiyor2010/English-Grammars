Stative Verbs

Rule

Stative verbs describe states (feelings, thoughts, possession, senses). They are not usually used in continuous tenses.

✔ I like this song.
✖ I am liking this song.

Important Note

Some verbs can be continuous when describing actions:

  • think – I am thinking about the exam.
  • see – I am seeing a doctor.
  • smell – The chef is smelling the soup.
  • taste – I am tasting the sauce.
  • look – She is looking at the painting.
  • feel – I am feeling the fabric.
  • have – I am having lunch.
  • love – I am loving this experience.
