Countable and Uncountable Nouns

Countable Nouns


Countable nouns are nouns that we can count. They have singular and plural forms and can take singular or plural verbs.

  • There are two restaurants near my house.
  • I met some interesting people at the party.
  • We need a few chairs for the meeting.
  • Several students asked the teacher a question.
  • She bought three tickets for the concert.

How to Make Regular Nouns Plural

Rule Example Sentence
Add -s book → books I bought two books yesterday.
Add -es (after s, sh, ch, x, z) box → boxes There are three boxes on the table.
Consonant + y → ies baby → babies The babies are sleeping.
Vowel + y → add -s toy → toys The children are playing with toys.
f / fe → ves wolf → wolves Wolves live in forests.
o → +es (some words) tomato → tomatoes She bought fresh tomatoes.

Helpful Hints

  • a / an — I bought a car.
  • many — She has many friends.
  • a few — We have a few questions.
  • several — Several students were late.
  • one / two / three — There are three books on the desk.
  • these / those — Those buildings are very old.
  • each / every — Each student received a certificate.

Uncountable Nouns


Uncountable nouns are nouns that we cannot count individually. They do not have a plural form and usually take a singular verb.

  • She gave me some useful advice.
  • My money is in my wallet.
  • The news was a complete shock.
  • I need more information about the exam.
  • There is a lot of water in the bottle.

Here are simple rules to know whether a noun is countable or uncountable:

  • Countable: You can count it. Has singular and plural forms. Use numbers and a/an with singular.
    Examples: one apple, three cars, a book.

  • Uncountable: You cannot count it individually. No plural form. Often refers to liquids, materials, abstract ideas.
    Examples: water, sugar, advice, furniture, happiness.

Watch Out

  • We do NOT say: ✘ advices / informations / moneys.
  • Correct: ✔ advice, information, money (no -s).
  • News looks plural but takes a singular verb: ✔ The news is good.
  • Clothes and jeans are always plural: ✔ The clothes are on the bed.
  • Paper (material) is uncountable, but a paper (newspaper/article) is countable.

Helpful Hints

  • a little — I have a little money left.
  • much — There isn’t much information available.
  • a bit of — She gave me a bit of advice.
  • a piece of — He shared a piece of news.
  • a lot of / lots of — We received a lot of information.
  • the — The information was very helpful.
