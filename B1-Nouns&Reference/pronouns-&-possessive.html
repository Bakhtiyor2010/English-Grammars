Pronouns and Possessive Determiners

Subject Pronouns

Form: I / you / he / she / it / we / they

Use: Used as the subject of a verb (who does the action).

Pronoun Example
I I am learning programming.
You You speak English very well.
He He plays football every day.
She She studies computer science.
It It looks interesting.
We We are ready for the exam.
They They built the first aeroplane.

Object Pronouns

Form: me / you / him / her / it / us / them

Use: Used as the object of a verb or preposition (who receives the action).

Pronoun Example
me She helped me yesterday.
you I called you last night.
him We saw him at school.
her They invited her to the party.
it I bought it online.
us The teacher gave us homework.
them Can you help them?

Possessive Determiners

Form: my / your / his / her / its / our / their

Use: Show ownership. Always followed by a noun.

Pronoun Example
my This is my laptop.
your Is that your phone?
his That is his car.
her I like her dress.
its The dog wagged its tail.
our This is our classroom.
their That is their house.

Possessive Pronouns

Form: mine / yours / his / hers / ours / theirs

Use: Show ownership. NOT followed by a noun.

Pronoun Example
mine This book is mine.
yours The decision is yours.
his The jacket is his.
hers The idea was hers.
ours The victory is ours.
theirs The house is theirs.
Note: There is no possessive pronoun form of "it".

Reflexive Pronouns

Form: myself / yourself / himself / herself / itself / ourselves / yourselves / themselves

Use: Used when the subject and object are the same, or for emphasis.

Pronoun Example
myself I fixed it myself.
yourself Take care of yourself.
himself He taught himself programming.
herself She prepared herself for the exam.
itself The computer shut down itself.
ourselves We enjoyed ourselves at the party.
yourselves You should protect yourselves.
themselves They solved the problem themselves.
