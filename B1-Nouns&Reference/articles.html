Articles

a (indefinite article)

Use: singular countable nouns (not specific)

Examples:

  • I need to buy a book for school.
  • She wants to adopt a cat.
  • He bought a car last week.
  • Can I have a cup of coffee?
  • We stayed in a small hotel in the city center.

an (indefinite article)

Use: instead of "a" when the next word begins with a vowel sound

Examples:

  • I need an umbrella because it’s raining.
  • She is an honest person.
  • He ate an apple for breakfast.
  • They stayed in an expensive hotel.
  • He wants to be an engineer.
Watch out! Use a/an depending on the sound, not spelling.
Examples: an hour, a university

the (definite article)

Use: specific nouns

Examples:

  • Let's go to the park this afternoon.
  • Have you seen the movie I told you about?
  • She gave me the keys to her house.
  • We visited the Eiffel Tower in Paris.
  • He turned off the light before leaving.

Use Example
singular countable nouns (specific) Let's go to the new shopping centre.
plural countable nouns (specific) Where are the books I ordered?
uncountable nouns (specific) I gave the shop assistant the money and then left.

No article (zero article)

Use: general nouns

Examples:

  • Fresh fruit is good for your health.
  • Teachers help students learn.
  • I like reading books.
  • Children love playing outside.
  • Water is essential for life.

Use Example
plural countable nouns (general) Prices have gone up a lot recently.
uncountable nouns (general) Fresh fruit is really good for you.

Special Rules

1. Places

  • the: seas (the Atlantic), rivers (the Amazon), areas (the Antarctic), some countries (the USA, the UK), public buildings (the theatre), the Earth, the world, the sky, the moon, the sun, the sea, the environment
  • no article: towns and cities (Moscow), most countries (France), continents (Europe), streets (Baker Street), planets (Mars)

Examples:

  • I want to visit the USA next summer.
  • The ship is sailing across the Atlantic.
  • Paris is a beautiful city.
  • Mars is called the Red Planet.
  • We live near Baker Street.

2. Activities

  • a/an: have a job, work as a …
  • the: on the radio, the media, play the piano
  • no article: go to work, on TV, go shopping, play tennis, listen to music, go to school, be at school/university, school subjects (maths)

Examples:

  • He has a part-time job.
  • She is working as a teacher.
  • We listened to the radio all morning.
  • I like to go shopping on weekends.
  • They play tennis every Sunday.

3. Time

  • the: in the morning/afternoon/evening, on the 20th March, in the 1950s
  • no article: days (Thursday), months (May), years (2009), at night

Examples:

  • She arrived in the morning.
  • We met on Thursday.
  • He was born in 2009.
  • The festival starts on the 20th March.
  • I usually read at night.

4. People

  • the: the King, the Prime Minister, the army, the navy, the police, the Germans, the English
  • no article: become king, he’s English, speak English

Examples:

  • The Prime Minister gave a speech today.
  • The police are investigating the case.
  • He wants to become king one day.
  • She speaks English fluently.
  • The Germans enjoy football.
