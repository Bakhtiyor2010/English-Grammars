Present Perfect Simple

Form

Type Structure
Statement (I / You / We / They) + have + verb 3
(He / She / It) + has + verb 3
Negative (I / You / We / They) + haven't (have not) + verb 3
(He / She / It) + hasn't (has not) + verb 3
Question Have + (I / you / we / they) + verb 3?
Has + (he / she / it) + verb 3?

Expressions

  • for — I have lived here for five years.
  • since — She has worked here since 2020.
  • just — I have just eaten lunch.
  • already — They have already finished.
  • yet — Have you finished yet?
  • ever — Have you ever visited Paris?
  • never — I have never seen snow.
  • recently — She has recently moved.
  • lately — I have been tired lately.
  • so far — We have learned a lot so far.

Uses & Examples

Situations that started in the past and are still true

  • I have lived in Tashkent for ten years.
  • She has worked at this company since 2015.
  • We have known each other since childhood.
  • He has studied English for five years.
  • They have been friends for a long time.

Completed actions at a time in the past which is not mentioned

  • I have visited London.
  • She has tried sushi before.
  • We have seen that movie.
  • He has read that book.
  • They have traveled to Italy.

Completed actions where the important thing is the result now

  • I have lost my keys. (I can’t find them now)
  • She has broken her phone. (It is not working now)
  • We have finished the project. (The project is ready now)
  • He has cleaned his room. (It is tidy now)
  • They have bought a new car. (They own it now)

Grammar Note:

We don't use Present Perfect Simple when we say when something happened in the past. We use Past Simple.

  • ❌ I have met him yesterday. → ✅ I met him yesterday.
  • ❌ She has finished the work last night. → ✅ She finished the work last night.
  • ❌ They have moved here in 2020. → ✅ They moved here in 2020.

We don't use Past Simple when we want to show that something happened before now or is still important now. We use Present Perfect Simple.

  • ❌ I lost my keys. (but I don't have them now) → ✅ I have lost my keys.
  • ❌ She went home. (she is not here now) → ✅ She has gone home.
  • ❌ We finished the project. (result matters now) → ✅ We have finished the project.

Sentence Meaning Time Focus
I have finished my homework. The action happened before now and is important now Now
I finished my homework yesterday. The action happened at a specific time in the past Past time
