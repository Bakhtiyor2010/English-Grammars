Past Perfect Continuous

Form

Type Structure
Statement (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + had been + verb ing
Negative (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + hadn't (had not) been + verb ing
Question Had + (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) been + verb ing?

Expressions

  • for — He had been studying English for two hours before lunch.
  • since — She had been working at that company since 2010.
  • before — They had been playing football before it started raining.
  • all day/night — I had been reading all day before the visitors came.
  • just — He had just been cooking dinner when the phone rang.

Uses & Examples

Actions continuing up to, or stopping just before, a moment in the past

  • She had been studying for three hours before her friends called.
  • They had been playing football all afternoon when it started raining.
  • I had been working at the company for five years before I got promoted.
  • He had been waiting for the bus for 30 minutes when it finally arrived.
  • We had been walking in the park when it suddenly got dark.

Grammar Note:

The past perfect simple often emphasises the result of an action:

  • She had finished her homework, so she could watch TV. (Focus on the finished homework → result)
  • He had cleaned the room before the guests arrived. (Result: room is ready)
  • They had built the bridge by last year. (Result: the bridge exists)

The past perfect continuous often emphasises the action, and the time spent on the action, rather than the result:

  • She had been studying for three hours before her friends called. (Focus on the studying process and duration)
  • He had been painting the house all day, so he was very tired. (Emphasizes effort/time spent)
  • They had been walking for miles when it started raining. (Focus on the ongoing activity and duration)

Sentence Meaning Focus
I had worked all day. The action is completed; result is important now. Result
I had been working all day. The action is continuous; emphasis on time spent. Action / Duration
