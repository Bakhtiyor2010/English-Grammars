Form

Type Structure

(He / She / It) + verb (s / es)

(He / She / It) + doesn't (does not) + verb
Question Do + (I / you / we / they) + verb?

Expressions

Common Adverbs of Frequency

  • always — I always brush my teeth in the morning.
  • usually — She usually takes the bus to school.
  • normally — We normally have lunch at 1 pm.
  • often — He often goes for a walk after dinner.
  • frequently — I frequently visit my grandparents.
  • sometimes — They sometimes watch movies on weekends.
  • occasionally — She occasionally drinks coffee in the evening.
  • rarely — He rarely eats fast food.
  • seldom — We seldom go to the cinema.
  • never — I never skip my homework.

Time Phrases

  • every day / week / month / year — I go to the gym every day.
  • every Monday / Friday — I have piano lessons every Monday.
  • in the morning / afternoon / evening — She studies in the afternoon.
  • at night — He often goes for a walk at night.
  • on weekends — We visit our grandparents on weekends.
  • once a day / week / month — I call my best friend once a week.
  • twice a week — We practice football twice a week.
  • three times a month — She goes to the cinema three times a month.
  • from time to time — He travels abroad from time to time.

Uses & Examples

Present Habits

  • I drink coffee every morning.
  • She goes to the gym on Mondays.
  • They play video games after school.
  • We usually eat dinner at 7 p.m.
  • He reads before bed.

Permanent Situations

  • She lives in London.
  • My father works in a bank.
  • They own a small shop.
  • We study at the same school.
  • He drives a taxi.

States (feelings, thoughts)

  • I like this song.
  • She loves chocolate.
  • They believe your story.
  • We understand the lesson.
  • He wants a new phone.

General Truths

  • The sun rises in the east.
  • Water freezes at 0°C.
  • Cats like milk.
  • The Earth moves around the Sun.
  • Light travels fast.

Timetables

  • The train leaves at 9:15 tomorrow morning.
  • Our school starts at 8:00 every weekday.
  • The bus arrives at the station at 6:30 p.m. tonight.
  • The flight departs at noon on Friday.
  • The concert begins at 7:00 next Saturday.

Helpful Hint

Adverbs of frequency usually come BEFORE the main verb, but AFTER the verb "to be".

  • I often play football.
  • She always wakes up early.
  • They sometimes watch TV.
  • I am always late.
  • He is usually tired.
