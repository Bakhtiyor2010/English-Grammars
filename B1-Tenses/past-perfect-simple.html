Past Perfect Simple

Form

Type Structure
Statement (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + had + verb 3
Negative (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + hadn't (had not) + verb 3
Question Had + (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + verb 3?

Expressions

  • by — I had finished my homework by eight o’clock.
  • by the time — By the time I arrived, the lesson had started.
  • before — The teacher had checked the answers before the lesson.
  • after — I had left after I had finished the test.
  • just — Simon had just finished the test when the bell rang.
  • when — I left when I had finished the test.

Uses & Examples

Actions and states before a moment in the past

  • She had finished her homework before her friends arrived.
  • By the time I called, he had already left the office.
  • They had known each other for years before they got married.
  • The train had departed when we reached the station.
  • He had lived in London before moving to Paris.

Finished actions and states where the important thing is the result at a moment in the past

  • I had lost my keys, so I couldn’t get into the house.
  • She had cooked dinner before her guests arrived.
  • They had built the new bridge by the end of last year.
  • He had written the report, so his boss was happy.
  • We had cleaned the house before the party started.

Grammar Note:

Whether we use the past simple or the past perfect simple can change the meaning of a sentence.

  • I ate breakfast before I went to school. (Simple sequence of events)
  • I had eaten breakfast before I went to school. (Focus on the fact breakfast was already done)
  • She finished the book last night. (Focus on when it happened)
  • She had finished the book before the teacher asked for it. (Emphasizes result before another past action)
  • He left the party early. (Just states the action in the past)
  • He had left the party when I arrived. (Shows he was already gone at that past moment)

Sentence Meaning Time Focus
I had finished my homework before dinner. The homework was completed first, then dinner happened. Past sequence
I finished my homework yesterday. Focus on the past action, sequence less emphasized. Past action
