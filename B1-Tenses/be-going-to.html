Be Going To

Form

Type Structure
Statement I + am + going to + verb
(He / She / It) + is + going to + verb
(You / We / They) + are + going to + verb
Negative I + am not + going to + verb
(He / She / It) + isn't (is not) + going to + verb
(You / We / They) + aren't (are not) + going to + verb
Question Am + I + going to + verb?
Is + (he / she / it) + going to + verb?
Are + (you / we / they) + going to + verb?

Expressions

Time Expressions

  • tomorrow — I am going to start my new course tomorrow.
  • next week / month / year — She is going to visit her friend next week.
  • soon — He is going to fix the computer soon.
  • in a few minutes / hours / days — We are going to leave in a few minutes.
  • later — They are going to paint the room later.

Uses & Examples

Intentions

  • I am going to start a new course next week.
  • She is going to visit her friend tomorrow.
  • We are going to buy a new car this month.
  • He is going to call his parents tonight.
  • They are going to paint the house this weekend.

Predictions (often with evidence we can see)

  • Look at those dark clouds. It is going to rain soon.
  • The sky is clear. The sun is going to shine all day.
  • He is coughing a lot. He is going to get sick.
  • The team is leading in points. They are going to win the match.
  • The traffic lights are red. The cars are going to stop.

Facts about the Future

  • The train is going to leave at 8:00 a.m. tomorrow.
  • School is going to start on Monday.
  • The meeting is going to begin at 10:00 a.m.
  • The plane is going to arrive at 6:00 p.m.
  • Winter is going to come next December.
