Future Simple

Form

Type Structure
Statement (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + will + verb
(I / We) + shall + verb
Negative (I / You / We / They / He / She / It) + will not (won't) + verb
(I / We) + shall not (shan't) + verb
Question Will + (I / you / we / they / he / she / it) + verb?
Shall + (I / we) + verb?

Expressions

Time Expressions

  • tomorrow — I will call you tomorrow.
  • next week / month / year — She will visit us next week.
  • soon — He will finish the work soon.
  • in a few minutes / hours / days — We will leave in a few minutes.
  • later — They will start the project later.
  • in the future — I will travel more in the future.

Uses & Examples

Facts about the Future

  • The sun will rise at 6:30 tomorrow.
  • Winter will come next December.
  • The train will leave at 8:00 a.m. tomorrow.
  • School will start on Monday.
  • The meeting will begin at 10:00 a.m.

Predictions

  • I think it will rain tomorrow.
  • She will become a great doctor.
  • He will pass the exam without any problems.
  • They will arrive late because of the traffic.
  • The team will win the championship this year.

Offers and Requests

  • Will you help me with my homework?
  • I will carry your bag for you.
  • Shall I open the window?
  • Will you come to my party tomorrow?
  • I will make some tea for you.

Decisions Made Now

  • I'm tired. I will go to bed early tonight.
  • We will order pizza for dinner.
  • It's cold. I will put on my jacket.
  • I forgot to call her. I will do it now.
  • The phone is ringing. I will answer it.

Helpful Hints

With offers which are questions, we use Shall with I and we.

  • Shall I open the window?
  • Shall we go to the park?
  • Shall I help you with the cleaning?

We do not use will for arrangements.

  • We'll visit my grandma this weekend.
  • I'll meet you at 5 p.m. tomorrow.
  • We'll start the meeting at 10 a.m.
